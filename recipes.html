Whole Wheat Bread

Origin: Fertile Crescent Source: Family Recipe Category: Main course

Online resources from Simple Recipes

Recipe Ingredients

  • Unsalted butter
  • Milk
  • Whole wheat flour
  • All-purpose flour

Recipe Steps

  1. Warm the milk in the microwave, stirring in between, just to take the chill off.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk whole wheat flour, bread flour, and salt.
  3. Pour the milk and yeast mixture into well in the flour.
  4. Add the butter and honey mixture.
  5. As you knead, the dough should feel soft, pliable, and a little tacky.
  6. Cover with plastic wrap or a clean dish towel and let it sit on the counter until it has just about doubled in size.
  7. Gently flatten the dough with the tips of your fingers to form a puffy oval roughly the width of your baking pan.
  8. Preheat the oven to 375°F halfway through second rise and bake the loaves.
  9. When the loaves have finished baking, remove from oven and baste the tops with butter one more time.

Kung Pao Chicken

Origin: Beijing Source: Family Recipe Category: Main course

This recipe comes from my mother. Because Kung Pao Chicken is a very common dish in Beijing. When I’m not at home, I often think of Kung Pao Chicken made at home.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Skinless chicken breasts
  • Unsalted peanuts
  • Dried red chili peppers
  • Green bell pepper
  • Red bell pepper
  • Diced scallions
  • Garlic
  • Fresh ginger
  • Soy sauce
  • Fresh ginger
  • Rice wine
  • Fresh ginger
  • Cornstarch
  • Rice vinegar
  • Hoisin sauce

Recipe Steps

  1. Combine the chicken with the marinade ingredients: soy sauce, rice wine (or sherry), and cornstarch.
  2. Mix together all the sauce ingredients: soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, sugar, cornstarch, and chicken broth.
  3. Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry until it turns white and is nearly cooked through.
  4. Add the minced garlic, ginger, and chopped dried red chili peppers.
  5. Return the cooked chicken to the wok and stir everything together.
  6. Add the unsalted peanuts.
  7. Add the diced scallions and stir for another minute.

Avocado toast

Origin: Australia Source: School dining service Category: Breakfast

I used to make avocado toast when I was working in a School cafeteria. I think it is relatively simple to make and nutritious, so I will also choose it as my breakfast.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Avocado
  • Whole wheat
  • Salt and pepper
  • Red pepper flakes
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon juice

Recipe Steps

  1. Toast the Bread.
  2. Cut the ripe avocado in half, remove the pit, and scoop out the flesh into a bowl.
  3. Use a fork to mash the avocado in the bowl. You can make it as smooth or as chunky as you prefer.
  4. For a subtle garlic flavor, cut a clove of garlic in half and rub the cut side on the toasted bread slices.
  5. Spread the mashed avocado evenly onto the toasted bread slices.
  6. Add A drizzle of olive oil and lemon juices
