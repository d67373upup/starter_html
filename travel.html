New York

Geographical Location: North America

New York, part of New York State in the United States, is the largest city in the United States. It is located at the mouth of the Hudson River on the northeastern coast of the United States, on the edge of the Atlantic Ocean, and has a temperate continental humid climate.

The city was born in 1613 as a small Dutch settlement on Manhattan Island called "New Amsterdam". In 1664, the British purchased the island for $24 and renamed it New York to commemorate that time.

Columbus

Geographical Location: North America

Columbus is the capital and largest city of the U.S. state of Ohio. It is centrally located within the state and serves as the economic, cultural, and political hub of Ohio. Columbus is known for its vibrant and diverse community, featuring a mix of urban and suburban neighborhoods.

Columbus and The Ohio State University contribute to the cultural, economic, and educational vitality of the region, making the city a dynamic and welcoming place for residents and visitors alike.

Hong Kong

Geographical Location: Asia

Hong Kong is a dynamic and vibrant metropolis located on the southern coast of China. It is renowned for its stunning skyline, bustling harbor, rich history, and unique blend of Eastern and Western cultures.

Hong Kong's cultural heritage is deeply influenced by its colonial past (under British rule until 1997) and its Chinese roots. This fusion is evident in its cuisine, architecture, festivals, and way of life.

Tsingtao

Geographical Location: Asia

Tsingtao is a coastal city in eastern China, situated along the shores of the Yellow Sea. With its captivating blend of natural beauty, historical significance, and modern development, Qingdao is a city that offers a rich and diverse experience.

Tsingtao has a unique history influenced by both Chinese and foreign cultures. It was once a German colonial settlement and later came under Japanese occupation before returning to Chinese sovereignty. The remnants of its colonial past can still be seen in its architecture and culture.

Xi'an

Geographical Location: Asia

Xi'an, located in the north-central part of China's Shaanxi Province, is one of the country's oldest and most historically significant cities. With a rich heritage that spans thousands of years, Xi'an is celebrated for its cultural treasures, ancient relics, and as the eastern terminus of the Silk Road.

Xi'an is one of the Four Great Ancient Capitals of China, boasting a history dating back over 3,000 years. It served as the capital of several Chinese dynasties, including the Qin, Han, and Tang, making it a center of political, cultural, and economic influence.

Xiaoqikong Town

Geographical Location: Asia

Xiaoqikong Town (小七孔镇) is a picturesque and tranquil town located in the Libo County of Qiannan Buyi and Miao Autonomous Prefecture in Guizhou Province, China. It's renowned for its stunning karst landscape, lush forests, crystal-clear rivers, and unique geological formations.

Xiaoqikong is nestled in a karst region, known for its limestone formations that have been eroded over millions of years to create breathtaking natural wonders. The town is part of the larger Libo karst cluster, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
